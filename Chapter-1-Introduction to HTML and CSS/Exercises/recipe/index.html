Grape Leaves (Dolmas)

grape leaves, also known as Dolmas, are a traditional Mediterranean dish filled with a flavorful rice and herb mixture. These tender and savory bites are perfect as appetizers or part of a mezze platter.

Ingredients

  • 1 jar grape leaves in brine, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup long grain rice
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (plus more for drizzling)
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth

Method

  1. In a pan, heat olive oil and sauté chopped onions until soft.
  2. Add rice, herbs, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes, then add 1 cup of water and simmer until water is absorbed. Let it cool.
  3. Place a grape leaf shiny side down, add a spoonful of filling in the center, fold sides in, and roll tightly.
  4. Place rolled dolmas seam-side down in a pot, packing them tightly in layers.
  5. Pour remaining water and lemon juice over the dolmas. Drizzle with olive oil and place a plate on top to keep them in place.
  6. Simmer gently for 45–60 minutes until leaves are tender and rice is fully cooked.
  7. Let cool to room temperature. Serve with lemon wedges or yogurt.
